# CODEX Code of Conduct

## Our standard

Participation in CODEX requires respectful, evidence-oriented, and constructive collaboration.

Contributors are expected to:

- criticize ideas and documents rather than people;
- distinguish fact, hypothesis, interpretation, and preference;
- acknowledge uncertainty and correction;
- respect linguistic, religious, philosophical, and cultural differences;
- provide attribution and disclose conflicts of interest;
- avoid harassment, intimidation, discrimination, and deliberate misrepresentation;
- protect private information and unpublished materials.

## Scholarly disagreement

Disagreement is expected. It should be documented through evidence, alternative readings, and explicit consequences. No contributor is required to accept a claim solely because of status or seniority.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, lock, or remove contributions that violate this code and may restrict participation when necessary. Significant enforcement actions should be documented privately or publicly as appropriate, while protecting affected individuals.

## Scope

This code applies to repository discussions, issues, pull requests, reviews, project communications, and public representation of CODEX.
